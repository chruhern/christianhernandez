FLARE (2025)

Power Electronics, PCB Design, and Rapid Prototyping

FLARE is LiDAR sensing hardware our team designed to help analyze below canopy fuel loads by packaging the electronics and software required to periodically capture LiDAR point cloud data in an integrated and easy-to-use system for researchers and scientist.

Wildfires cost the U.S. up to $893 billion each year, yet our understanding of fire-preventive strategies is not fully developed, especially when it comes to understanding how fires spread on the ground level. The solution to this problem involves understanding fuel loads, specifically how well we can analyze fuel loads below the tree canopy level. This is the problem Silvaye, a climate tech startup, aimed to solve with our help, pitching LiDAR as the below-canopy sensor ideal for filling this hole in data collection.

Our team, comprised of five memebers tackled this design problem for our senior design project at UC Santa Cruz, delivering a compact device capable of starting a scan remotely, meeting the needs of our client. More information can be found on our team website here.
