Field Navigating Robot (2024)

Filter Design, Sensor and Actuator Integration, and Power Electronics

ECE-118 is a ten-week course at UC Santa Cruz that culminates with solving an open-ended mechatronics problem in a three-person team. This year, we were tasked with designing an autonomous robot that can robustly navigate an obstacle-filled playing field, locating and trapping rolling 25mm chrome balls, storing them within our robot in order to push them onto our opponents field all within a two-minute round.

To effectively complete the challenge, our team split into three sub-teams: mechanical, electrical, and software. I led the electrical sub-team, designing the fourth-order butterworth bandpass filter required to detect a 2KHz signal, the robots power distribution board, and developed the wiring harness for our actuators and sensors. Our final design consisted of a square chassis fitted with a motorized vaccuum-style intake for collection and disposal, a retractable servo-arm to open the opponents door, and micro-switches for bump/obstacle detection. Inside the PIC32, our software lead designed a hierarchical state machine programmed with event detection capabilities to handle field-navigation. We succesfully completed the challenge and finished second in competition.
